Congratulations! You may attend HackRU!

We can't wait to see you, but before that, please read through this email which contain some important information.

Confirmation

You are currently confirmed to attend, however if you change your mind, please log into your Dashboard and click the button that says Change Confirmation Status and select Not Attending.

Waiver

Print and complete this waiver and bring it with you. If you are a Rutgers student under 18, it must be signed by a parent or guardian. Any participant without a waiver will be denied entry per university policy. Any non-Rutgers student under 18 may not attend and will be turned away upon arrival.

Check-In

Check-in for HackRU begins at 10am and continues until 12pm, when opening ceremonies begin. If you do not arrive before 12pm, you may not be guaranteed a spot since we will be allowing wait-listed registrants in after 12pm. Admission is permitted based on current capacity of the event. Entry into the event, even with confirmed attendance, is not guaranteed.

Schedule

A schedule for the weekend can be found on the website. A more detailed schedule will be released on the day of the event. Announcements will be posted on social media - like and follow us on Facebook so you don't miss out on awesome mini-events.

Getting Here

The address to the Rutgers Athletic Center (RAC, and also known as the Louis Brown Athletic Center) is 83 Rockafeller Rd, Piscataway Township, NJ 08854. Visitors may park in the Scarlet, Green, or Yellow Lots without permits. Rutgers students with valid permits may park overnight in the Yellow Lot. Maps for the parking lots can be found here. Park only in the designated lots to avoid being ticketed. We will be unable to dispute any tickets outside of the designated lots. Also note, that if you are a Rutgers student without a valid parking permit and attempt to park in these lots, you can be ticketed.

If you're arriving from the train station, head to the nearest Rutgers bus stop (Train Station stop). Wait for the "Weekend 1" or "Weekend 2" bus. It is completely free to get on a Rutgers bus. Downloading the official Rutgers mobile app can help track when the bus arrives. Get off the bus at the "Livingston Plaza" stop. Upon stepping off the bus, turn right and walk down the street and the large Rutgers Athletic Center will be on your right.

What to Bring

  • signed waiver
  • laptop, charger, other hacking tools
  • ID with picture and date of birth (required for non-Rutgers students)
  • student ID (if you're a Rutgers student)
  • toiletries
  • blanket, pillow, other sleeping things
What NOT to Bring
  • weapons or anything that looks like a weapon
  • alcohol or illegal substances
  • quadcopters/drones/etc.
  • animals
  • "hoverboards"
  • basically, use your common sense - if you're not sure, ask us!

Miscellaneous

Make sure to like us on Facebook, RSVP to our Facebook event page, and follow us on Twitter and Instagram to make sure you don't miss any important updates!

If you have any questions, please feel free to email us at info@hackru.org or message us on Facebook. See you at HackRU!
